This module gives information about the unipotent conjugacy classes of a connected reductive group рқҗҶ over an algebraically closed field k of characteristic p, and various associated invariants, such as the generalised Springer correspondence. The unipotent classes depend on p; their classification differs when p is not good (that is, when p divides one of the coefficients of the highest root of one of the irreducible components). In good characteristic, the unipotent classes are in bijection with nilpotent orbits on the Lie algebra рқ”Ө of рқҗҶ.

For each unipotent conjugacy class, we give the following information for a representative u of that class:

  • the centralizer $C_рқҗҶ (u)$, that we describe by the reductive part of $C_рқҗҶ (u)^0$, by the group of components $A(u):=C_рқҗҶ (u)/C_рқҗҶ (u)^0$, and by the dimension of its unipotent radical.

  • in good characteristic, the Dynkin-Richardson diagram.

  • the generalised Springer correspondence, attaching a character of the Weyl group or a relative Weyl group to each character of A(u).

The Dynkin-Richarson diagram is attached to a nilpotent element $e$ of the Lie algebra $рқ”Ө$. By the Jacobson-Morozov theorem there exists an $рқ”°рқ”©вӮӮ$ subalgebra of $рқ”Ө$ containing $e$ as the element $\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\0&0 \end{pmatrix}$. Let $рқҗ’$ be the torus $\begin{pmatrix}h&0\\0&h^{-1} \end{pmatrix}$ of $SLвӮӮ$ and let $рқҗ“$ be a maximal torus containing $рқҗ’$, so that $рқҗ’$ is the image of a one-parameter subgroup $ПғвҲҲ Y(рқҗ“)$. Consider the root decomposition $рқ”Ө=вҲ‘_{ОұвҲҲОҰ}рқ”Ө_Оұ$ given by $рқҗ“$ and the root system ОҰ; then $ОұвҶҰвҹЁПғ,Оұвҹ©$ defines a linear form on $ОҰ$, determined by its value on simple roots О . It is possible to choose a system of simple roots such that $вҹЁПғ,Оұвҹ©вүҘ 0$ for $ОұвҲҲО $, and then $вҹЁПғ,Оұвҹ©вҲҲ{0,1,2}$ for any $ОұвҲҲО $. The Dynkin diagram of $О $ decorated by these values $0,1,2$ is called the Dynkin-Richardson diagram of $e$, and in good characteristic is a complete invariant of the $рқ”Ө$-orbit of $e$. The Dynkin-Richardson diagrams thus classify unipotent classes of рқҗҶ in good characteristic.

Another classification of unipotent classes was given by (Bala and Carter, 1976). A standard parabolic subgroup рқҗҸ of рқҗҶ associated with the subset IвҠӮО  of the simple roots is distinguished if the linear form Пғ taking the value 2 on ОұвҲҲ I and 0 on other simple roots satisfies 2nвӮҖ+semisimplerank(рқҗҶ)=nвӮӮ, where nбөў is the number of roots in ОҰ where Пғ takes the value i. Given a distinguished parabolic рқҗҸ, there is a unique unipotent class which is dense in the unipotent radical of рқҗҸ. For this class, the linear form described by the Dynkin-Richardson diagram is equal to Пғ. Such unipotent classes are called distinguished. The Bala-Carter theorem says that every unipotent class is distinguished in the smallest Levi subgroup рқҗӢ which contains it, and that such pairs of рқҗӢ and the distinguished parabolic рқҗҸ of рқҗӢ taken up to рқҗҶ-conjugacy are in bijection with unipotent classes of рқҗҶ. The function induced_linear_form allows to find the Dynkin-Richardson diagram in рқҗҶ of a unipotent class of рқҗӢ and thus recover these diagrams from the Bala-Carter classification.

We now describe the Springer correspondence. Indecomposable locally constant $рқҗҶ$-equivariant sheaves on a unipotent class $C$, called local systems, are parameterised by irreducible characters of $A(u)$ for uвҲҲ C. The ordinary Springer correspondence is a bijection between irreducible characters of the Weyl group and a large subset of the local systems, containing all trivial local systems (those parameterised by the trivial character of $A(u)$ for each $u$). More generally, the generalised Springer correspondence associates to each local system a (unique up to $рқҗҶ$-conjugacy) cuspidal datum, a Levi subgroup $рқҗӢ$ of $рқҗҶ$ and a cuspidal local system on an unipotent class of $рқҗӢ$, and a character of the relative Weyl group $W_рқҗҶ (рқҗӢ):=N_рқҗҶ (рқҗӢ)/рқҗӢ$. There are few cuspidal local systems (at most one in each dimension for classical groups). The ordinary Springer correspondence is the special case where $рқҗӢ$ is a maximal torus, the cuspidal local system is the trivial system on the identity element, and $W_рқҗҶ (рқҗӢ)=W$, the Weyl group.

The Springer correspondence gives information on the character values of a finite reductive group as follows: assume that $k$ is the algebraic closure of a finite field $рқ”Ҫ_q$ and that $F$ is the Frobenius attached to an $рқ”Ҫ_q$-structure of $рқҗҶ$. Let $C$ be an $F$-stable unipotent class and let $uвҲҲ C^F$; we call $C$ the geometric class of $u$ and the $рқҗҶ^F$-classes within $C^F$ are parameterised by the $F$-conjugacy classes of $A(u)$, denoted by $HВ№(F,A(u))$ (most of the time we can find $uвҲҲ C$ such that $F$ acts trivially on $A(u)$ and $HВ№(F,A(u))$ parametrises the conjugacy classes). To an $F$-stable character $ПҶ$ of $A(u)$ we associate the characteristic function of the corresponding local system (actually associated with an extension $ПҶМғ$ of $ПҶ$ to $A(u).F$); it is a class function $Y_{u,ПҶ}$ on $рқҗҶ^F$ which can be normalised so that: $Y_{u,ПҶ}(uвӮҒ)=ПҶМғ(cF)$ if $uвӮҒ$ is geometrically conjugate to $u$ and its $рқҗҶ^F$-class is parameterised by the $F$-conjugacy class $cF$ of $A(u)$, otherwise $Y_{u,ПҶ}(uвӮҒ)=0$. If the pair $u,ПҶ$ corresponds via the Springer correspondence to the character $ПҮ$ of $W_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ)$, then $Y_{u,ПҶ}$ is also denoted $YбөӘ$. There is another important class of functions indexed by local systems: to a local system on class $C$ is attached an intersection cohomology complex, which is a complex of sheaves supported on the closure $CМ„$. Such a complex of sheaves is associated with its characteristic function, a class function of $рқҗҶ^F$ obtained by taking the alternating trace of the Frobenius acting on the stalks of the cohomology sheaves. If $Y_ПҲ$ is the characteristic function of a local system, the characteristic function of the corresponding intersection cohomology complex is denoted by $X_ПҲ$. This function is supported on $CМ„$, and Lusztig has shown that $X_ПҲ=вҲ‘_ПҮ P_{ПҲ,ПҮ} YбөӘ$ where $P_{ПҲ,ПҮ}$ are integer polynomials in $q$ and $YбөӘ$ are attached to local systems on classes lying in $CМ„$.

Lusztig and Shoji have given an algorithm to compute the matrix $P_{ПҲ,ПҮ}$, which is implemented in Chevie. The relation to the characters of $рқҗҶ(рқ”Ҫ_q)$, considering for simplicity the ordinary Springer correspondence, is that the restriction to the unipotent elements of the almost character $R_ПҮ$ is equal to $q^{bбөӘ} XбөӘ$, where $bбөӘ$ is $dim в„¬бөӨ$ for an element u of the class C such that the support of ПҮ is $CМ„$. The restrictions of the Deligne-Lusztig characters $R_w$ for wвҲҲ W on the unipotents are called the Green functions and can also be computed by Chevie. The values of all unipotent characters on unipotent elements can also be computed in principle by applying Lusztig's Fourier transform matrix (see the section on the Fourier matrix) but there is a difficulty in that the $XбөӘ$ must first be multiplied by some roots of unity which are not known in all cases (and when known may depend on the congruence class of $q$ modulo some small prime).

Finally, we describe how unipotent classes of рқҗҶ are parameterised in various quasisimple groups. In classical types, the classes are parametrised by partitions corresponding to the Jordan form in the standard representation. Thus,

  • for AвӮҷ we have partitions of n+1.
  • for B_n we have partitions of 2n+1 where even parts occur an even number of times. In characteristic 2, types B and C are isogenous so have the same classification; thus see the next paragraph.
  • for C_n we have partitions of 2n where odd parts occur an even number of times. In characteristic 2, there are 2бөҸ classes attached to a partition where k is the number of even parts which occur an even number of times.
  • for D_n we have partitions of 2n where even parts occur an even number of times, excepted there are two classes when all parts are even. In characteristic 2, we have partitions of 2n where odd parts occur an even number of times, excepted there are 2бөҸ+Оҙ classes attached to a partition where k is the number of even parts which occur an even number of times, and Оҙ is 2 when all parts are even and 0 otherwise.

In exceptional groups, the names of the classes are derived from the Bala-Carter classification. The name of a class parametrised by (рқҗӢ,рқҗҸ) is of the form l(p) where l is the name of рқҗӢ and (p) is present if there is more than one distinguished parabolic in рқҗӢ and describes which one it is. Before the Bala-Carter classification was universally adopted, Shoji and Mizuno used a different scheme where a class was sometimes parametrised by a reductive subgroup of maximal rank which was not a Levi. These older labels can be obtained instead by giving the IO property :shoji=>true or :mizuno=>true. In a bad characteristic p, there are extra classes. Each of them is associated with a class c in good characteristic and is called (c)вӮҡ.

The data on unipotent classes for arbitrary reductive groups are obtained as follows. We may reduce to the case where the radical is trivial, that is рқҗҶ is semisimple. Then рқҗҶ is the quotient of a simply connected group by a finite central group. The data for quasi-simple simply connected groups have been entered by hand for each type and a general simply connected group is a direct product of quasi-simple ones. In such a group, a character of A(Z), the group of components of the centre, is attached to each Springer series. For an isogenous reductive group рқҗҶ' with centre Z' the group A(Z') is a quotient of the group A(Z). The Springer series for рқҗҶ' are those such that the corresponding character of A(Z) factors through A(Z') (to compute A(Z') see algebraic_center). The geometric unipotent classes of рқҗҶ and рқҗҶ' are in bijection. For u a unipotent element of рқҗҶ' (which we can also consider as a unipotent element of рқҗҶ) the group AвӮҒ=A(u) in рқҗҶ' is a quotient of A=A(u) in рқҗҶ which we can compute as follows: the Springer correspondence for рқҗҶ' tells us which characters of A survive in рқҗҶ'. Then A' is the quotient of A by the common kernel of these characters.

UnipotentClasses(W[,p])

W should be a FiniteCoxeterGroup record for a Weyl group or rootdatum describing a reductive algebraic group рқҗҶ. The function returns a record containing information about the unipotent classes of рқҗҶ in characteristic p (if omitted, p is assumed to be any good characteristic for рқҗҶ). This contains the following fields:

group: a pointer to W

p: the characteristic of the field for which the unipotent classes were computed. It is 0 for any good characteristic.

orderclasses: a list describing the Hasse diagram of the partial order induced on unipotent classes by the closure relation. That is .orderclasses[i] is the list of j such that $CМ„вұјвҠӢ CМ„бөў$ and there is no class $CвӮ–$ such that $CМ„вұјвҠӢ CМ„вӮ–вҠӢ CМ„бөў$.

classes: a list of records holding information for each unipotent class. See the help for UnipotentClass for a description of these records.

springerseries: a list of records, each of which describes a Springer series of рқҗҶ.

The records describing individual Springer series have the following fields:

levi:the indices of the reflections corresponding to the Levi subgroup рқҗӢ where lives the cuspidal local system О№ from which the Springer series is induced.

relgroup: The relative Weyl group $N_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О№)/рқҗӢ$. The first series is the principal series for which .levi=[] and .relgroup=W.

locsys: a list of length nconjugacy_classes(.relgroup), holding in i-th position a pair describing which local system corresponds to the i-th character of $N_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О№)$. The first element of the pair is the index of the concerned unipotent class u, and the second is the index of the corresponding character of A(u).

Z: the central character associated to the Springer series, specified by its value on the generators of the center.

A struct UnipotentClass representing the class C of a unipotent element u of the reductive group рқҗҶ with Weyl group W, contains always the following information

  • .name The name of C
  • .parameter A parameter describing C. Sometimes the same as .name; a partition describing the Jordan form, for classical groups.
  • .dimBu The dimension of the variety of Borel subgroups containing u.

A UnipotentClass contains also some of the following information (all of it for some types and some characteristics but sometimes much less)

  • .dynkin the Dynkin-Richardson diagram of C (a vector giving a weight 0, 1 or 2 to the simple roots).
  • .dimred the dimension of the reductive part of C_G(u).
  • .red a CoxeterCoset recording the type of the reductive part of C_G(u), with the twisting induced by the Frobenius if any.

ICCTable(uc,seriesNo=1;q=Pol())

This function gives the table of decompositions of the functions $X_О№$ in terms of the functions $Y_О№$. Here О№ is a рқҗҶ-equivariant local system on the class C of a unipotent element u. Such a local system is parametrised by the pair (u,П•) of u and a character of the group of components A(u) of $C_рқҗҶ (u)$. The function $Y_О№$ is the characteristic function of this local system and $X_О№$ is the characteristic function of the corresponding intersection cohomology complex on CМ„. The Springer correspondence says that the local systems can also be indexed by characters of a relative Weyl group. Since the coefficient of XбөӘ on Yбө© is 0 if ПҮ and ПҶ are not characters of the same relative Weyl group (are not in the same Springer series), the table is for one Springer series, specified by the argument 'seriesNo' (this defaults to 'seriesNo=1' which is the principal series). The decomposition multiplicities are graded, and are given as polynomials in one variable (specified by the argument q; if not given Pol() is assumed).

induced_linear_form(W, H, h)

This routine can be used to find the unipotent class in the reductive group with Weyl group W which contains a given unipotent class of a reductive subgroup of maximum rank represented by the reflection subgroup H of W.

The argument h is a linear form on the roots of H, given by its value on the simple roots; this linear form is extended to the roots of W by 0 on the orthogonal of the roots of H; and finally the resulting form is conjugated by an element of W so that it takes positive values on the simple roots. If the initial form describes a Dynkin-Richardson diagram for H, the result will describe a Dynkin-Richardson diagram for W.

Chevie.Ucl.special_pieces вҖ” Function

'special_pieces(<uc>)'

The special pieces form a partition of the unipotent variety of a reductive group рқҗҶ which was first defined in (Spaltenstein, 1982; chap. III) as the fibres of d^2, where d is a "duality map". Another definition is as the set of classes in the Zariski closure of a special class and not in the Zariski closure of any smaller special class, where a special class is a class such that the trivial local system on it corresponds to a special character by the Springer correspondence.

Since each Lusztig family of characters contains a unique speical character, special pieces are in bijection with lusztig families.

Each piece is a union of unipotent conjugacy classes so is represented in Chevie as a list of class numbers. Thus the list of special pieces is returned as a list of lists of class numbers. The list is sorted by increasing piece dimension, while each piece is sorted by decreasing class dimension, so that the special class is listed first.

julia> special_pieces(UnipotentClasses(W))

julia> special_pieces(UnipotentClasses(W,3))

 [4, 3, 2, 6]
