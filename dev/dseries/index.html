d-Harish-Chandra series

d-Harish-Chandra series describe unipotent l-blocks of a finite reductive group $рқҗҶ(рқ”Ҫ_q)$ for $l|ОҰ_d(q)$ (at least, when l is not too small which means mostly not a bad prime for рқҗҶ). Some of the facts stated below are still partly conjectural, we do not try to distinguish precisely what has been established and what is still conjectural.

If (рқҗӢ,О») is a d-cuspidal pair then the constituents of the Lusztig induced $R_рқҗӢ^рқҗҶ(О»)$ are called a d-Harish-Chandra series; they form the unipotent part of an l-block of $рқҗҶ^F$ (BrouГ© et al., 1993; 5.24). It is conjectured (BrouГ© and Michel, 1997; 5.7) (and proven in some cases) that the $рқҗҶ^F$-endomorphism algebra of the l-adic cohomology of the variety рқҗ— which defines the Lusztig induction is a d-cyclotomic Hecke algebra $H_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»)$ for the group $W_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»):=N_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»)/рқҗӢ$, which is a complex reflection group вҖ“- here d-cyclotomic means that the parameters of $H_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»)$ are monomials in q and that $H_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»)$ specializes to the algebra of $W_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»)$ for $qвҶҰО¶_d$.

It follows that the decomposition of the Lusztig induction is of the form $R_рқҗӢ^рқҗҶ(О»)=вҲ‘_{П•вҲҲIrr(W_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»))}(-1)^{nбө©} П•(1)Оібө©,$ where Оібө© is a unipotent character of рқҗҶ^F attached to П• and where nбө© is the degree $H^{nбө©}_c(рқҗ—)$ where Оібө© occurss; and further for any П• we have $R_рқҗӢ^рқҗҶ(О»)(1)= (-1)^{nбө©} Оібө©(1)Sбө©$ where Sбө© is the Schur element of the character of $H_рқҗҶ(рқҗӢ,О»)$ which deforms to П•. The function Series allows to explore a d-Harish-Chandra series.

cuspidal_data(W[,d[,ad]];proper=false,all=false)

returns named tuples (levi=LF,cuspidal=О»,d=d) where LF is a d-split Levi (with d-center of dimension ad if ad is given) and О» is a d-cuspidal character of LF. If d=1 this returns ordinary cuspidal characters. The character О» is given as its index in the list of unipotent characters. If d was given as an integer, it is returned as a Root1 representing E(d).

If the keyword proper=true is given, only the data where LF!=W (or equivalently ad>0) are returned.

If d is omitted, data for all d orders of eigenvalues of elements of W is returned. If in addition the keyword argument all=true is given, data for all eigenvalues of elements of W is returned.

Series(W, L, cuspidal, d)

Chevie.Symbols.ennola вҖ” Function

ennola(S::CharSymbol)

Ennola of e-symbol S (of content 1 or 0) The order of Ennola (order of center of reflection group) is computed automatically: it is e for content 1 and gcd(e,rank(S)) for content 0.

source

ennola(W[,z=E(gcd(degrees(W)))])

Let W be an irreducible spetsial reflection group or coset, and z the generator of the center of W, viewed as the root of unity E(gcd(degrees(W)))]). Let рқ”ҫ be the spets attached to W. A property checked case-by case is that, for a unipotent character Оі of рқ”ҫ with polynomial generic degree deg Оі(q) , deg Оі(zq) is equal to Вұdeg Оі'(q) for another unipotent character Оі'; ВұОі' is called the Ennola transform of Оі. For W a Weyl group, the spets рқ”ҫ is a finite reductive group, in which case z=-1 if -1 is in W and z=1 otherwise. The function returns the signed permutation e done by ennola on the unipotent degrees (as an SPerm of 1:length(UnipotentCharacters(W))).

The SPerm e is not uniquely determined by the degrees since two degrees may be equal, but is uniquely determined by some additional axioms that we do not recall here (they include a description of the Ennola-permutation in terms of the Z-based rings attached to each Lusztig family).

If a second argument z is given, it should be a power of the default z and the corresponding power of e is returned.

julia> ennola(rootdatum("3D4"))
SPerm{Int64}: (3,-4)(5,-5)(6,-6)(7,-8)

julia> ennola(complex_reflection_group(14))

The above example shows that it may happen that the order of z-Ennola (here 18) is greater than the order of z (here 6); this is related to the presence of irrationalities qв…“ in the character table of the spetsial Hecke algebra of W.

For a non-irreducible group, z-ennola is defined if z can be considered an element of the the centre of each irreducible component.
