Let W be an irreducible spetsial reflection group or coset, and z the generator of the center of W, viewed as the root of unity E(gcd(degrees(W)))]). Let ğ”¾ be the spets attached to W. A property checked case-by case is that, for a unipotent character Î³ of ğ”¾ with polynomial generic degree deg Î³(q) , deg Î³(zq) is equal to Â±deg Î³'(q) for another unipotent character Î³'; Â±Î³' is called the Ennola transform of Î³. For W a Weyl group, the spets ğ”¾ is a finite reductive group, in which case z=-1 if -1 is in W and z=1 otherwise. The function returns the signed permutation e done by ennola on the unipotent degrees (as an SPerm of 1:length(UnipotentCharacters(W))).
